1850 - 2019

Introduction

The NULL Hypothesis

The temperature of the planet Earth is directly correlated with the concentration of CO2 in the Earths atmosphere.

There a few simple rules when looking at data.

  • Correlation is not causation
  • Correlation is not causation
  • Correlation is not causation

Unfortunately, there isn't a great deal of data to go on. Surface temperatures have been measured for only a few hundred years to any degree of accuracy. Atmospheric CO2 levels are in an even greater perilous state than temperatures.

I have 2 simple data sets.

  • The monthly Temperature Maximum and Minimum from Oxford in the UK from 1850 - June 2019
  • The measurement of atmospheric concentration of CO2 from 1959 - 2018 from NOAA

Is it possible to generalise from the particular to the universal in terms of AGW? Well it must be. Everywhere on the planet must be getting hotter due to the theory of back radiation from a trace gas, CO2.

I will be using Python with its numerous Scientific and Statistical code libraries to help me. I will present the findings, with a full reference to the techniques and computer code used to arrive at conclusions.

Some thoughts on the results

  • A linear regreesion model fits the increase in CO2 accurately.
  • A linear regression model is useless at describing the TmaxC data for the month of July since 1850.
  • The maximum Temperature in July has been over 26o on 5 occassions. 1911,1921,1983,2006 and 2018.
  • A 25 year rolling average of the data for July shows a pattern of rising and falling temperatures.
  • A 25 year rolling average of mean anuual temperatures since 1850 shows a similar pattern of rising and falling.
  • A linear regression model for the annual mean temperatures cannot be used.
  • A sixth degree polynomial equation fits the 25 year rolling average of annual mean temperatures..
  • This data does not indicate a linear corelation of rising CO2 with temperature.
  • Modelling historical temperatures cannot predict future temperatures.
  • The 'Little ice age' has been widely documented. An expectation would be that temperatures would have risen after 1850. They did. But actually cooled from the early 1960's to the late 1980's before starting to rise again.
  • Great care is advised when looking at graphed data. The scale can make things seem worse or better as per Fig. 1.
